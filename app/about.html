HOW WE DO WHAT WE DO.

Professional esports are taking incredible steps in following the path of traditional sports. Professional gamers compete for millions of dollars in sold out stadiums around the world, like Madison Square Garden and the Mercedes-Benz Arena. In fact, esports attracts hundreds of millions of viewers, more than the NHL, and can even be found on television on ESPN and TBS.

But despite the incredible growth of professional esports, collegiate esports are hugely underdeveloped, leaving colleges to miss out on a massive opportunity.

EGF's responsibility is to provide the anchor for an industry that's given so much to so many. We combine the best aspects of the NCAA and ESPN to bring varsity eSports programs to colleges around the world, and train the next generation of eSports professionals. To that end, we focus on three areas of operation:

DEVELOPMENT

EGF works directly with students and administrators to build esports programs on campuses, modeled to function like athletics programs.

COMPETITION

EGF organizes and oversees the esports leagues that those programs compete in, similar to traditional sports leagues.

MEDIA

EGF produces live broadcasts of all our matches, and creates original content.
